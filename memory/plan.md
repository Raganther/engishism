# Active Plan — "At Work" Lesson
Started: 2026-03-11

## Theme
"At Work" — B1 lesson on jobs vocabulary, modal verbs for obligation/permission, and fluency.
Distinct from creating-a-cv.js (CVs & interviews) — this focuses on describing jobs and workplace rules.
File: lessons/at-work.js

## Grammar focus
Modal verbs: have to / don't have to / can / can't / should / mustn't — in work contexts.

## Vocabulary focus
- Job sectors & types (healthcare, education, retail, construction, etc.)
- Adjectives to describe jobs (rewarding, demanding, well-paid, repetitive, flexible, stressful, creative, sociable)
- Compound nouns (working hours, sick pay, job title, pay rise, lunch break, dress code, shift work)

## Slides plan (17 activity types, all populated)

1. **title-card** — "At Work" / "Jobs, Vocabulary & Grammar — B1"

2. **reveal-card** — "Describing Your Job"
   - 8 adjectives: rewarding, demanding, well-paid, repetitive, flexible, stressful, creative, sociable
   - Each with a definition + example sentence

3. **reveal-card** — "Modal Verbs at Work"
   - have to / don't have to / can / can't / should / mustn't
   - Each with meaning + workplace example

4. **fill-blank** — Modal verbs in work sentences (6 questions, answers in [brackets])
   - e.g. "Nurses [have to] work long shifts." / "You [don't have to] wear a suit — casual is fine."

5. **meaning-pair** — Job description pairs: weak vs strong / obligation vs choice
   - e.g. "You must clean up." vs "You don't have to stay late." — discuss the difference

6. **true-false** — Workplace grammar & vocabulary (6 statements)
   - e.g. "A freelancer has to work 9–5." (False) / "A shift worker may work nights." (True)

7. **sentence-complete** — Complete sentences about jobs (5 stems)
   - e.g. "In my job I have to..." / "I don't have to..." / "I think the most rewarding job is..."

8. **hot-seat** — Job vocabulary (10 words: receptionist, shift, deadline, colleague, contractor, etc.)

9. **fluency-tree** — "What Do You Do?" conversation
   - A asks B about their job / ideal job
   - 3 paths: loves job / dislikes job / unemployed/student
   - Merge, then discuss ideal job
   - 8 turns, ends with open question about the future

10. **noughts-crosses** — 9 review questions (vocab + grammar)

11. **anagram** — 6 job/workplace words (SURGEON, WAITER, REWARDING, FLEXIBLE, DEADLINE, UNIFORM)

12. **call-my-bluff** — 3 tricky workplace terms (OVERTIME, SHIFT WORK, PROBATION... wait, probation used in cv lesson. Use: FLEXI-TIME, OVERTIME, OUTSOURCING)

13. **odd-one-out** — 4 sets
    - Job sectors, work adjectives, modal verbs, compound nouns

14. **missing-vowels** — 6 words: REWARDING, STRESSFUL, FLEXIBLE, DEMANDING, CREATIVE, SOCIABLE

15. **jeopardy** — 4 categories × 4 questions (200/400/600/800)
    - Describing Jobs / Modal Verbs / Job Sectors / Compound Nouns

16. **millionaire** — 10 questions on jobs vocab + modals, increasing difficulty

17. **countdown** — Free speaking (30s) — "Talk about a job you would love / hate."

18. **scenario-cards** — 6 workplace situations (modal verb decisions)
    - e.g. "Can you tell your boss you disagree?" / "Do you have to work unpaid overtime?"

19. **fluency-tree** — "Dream Job" — longer tree (3 paths, 8 turns, humour)
    - Paths: ambitious / realistic / funny/unexpected dream job
